<p><p>In the Graphics Display Terminal market, there are two main types: raster and vector. Raster displays use a grid of pixels to create images, resulting in high resolution but limited scalability. Vector displays, on the other hand, use mathematical formulas to create images, allowing for infinite scalability but lower resolution. Raster displays are more common in applications like computer monitors and televisions, while vector displays are often used in specialized applications such as CAD/CAM systems. Both types have their own strengths and weaknesses depending on the specific requirements of the user.</p></p>
