<p><p>Distress radio beacons come in three main types: Personal Locator Beacons (PLBs), which are carried by individuals and activated in emergencies to alert rescue authorities to their location; Emergency Position Indicating Radio Beacons (EPIRBs), which are installed on boats or aircraft and automatically transmit distress signals when activated; and Emergency Locator Transmitters (ELTs), which are designed for use in aircraft and send out distress signals in the event of a crash. Each type serves a specific purpose in alerting authorities to emergency situations and aiding in swift rescue operations.</p></p>
