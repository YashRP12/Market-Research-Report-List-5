<p><p>Dye mordants are substances that are used in the dyeing process to help fix the dye to the fabric or material being dyed. There are two main types of dye mordants: natural and synthetic.</p><p>Natural dye mordants are derived from plant or animal sources, such as tannins from oak galls or alum from minerals. These mordants have been used for centuries in traditional dyeing processes.</p><p>Synthetic dye mordants, on the other hand, are manufactured chemicals that serve the same purpose as natural mordants. They are often used in modern industrial dyeing processes for their consistency and reliability.</p></p>
