<p><p>Ultrasonic contrast media are substances used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of blood vessels and organs. There are two main types of contrast media: iodine-based and barium sulfate compounds. Iodine-based contrast media are commonly used in vascular imaging and for visualizing organs like the liver and kidneys. Barium sulfate compounds are used primarily for gastrointestinal imaging, as they are not absorbed by the body and provide excellent delineation of the digestive tract. Both types of contrast media play a crucial role in improving the accuracy and effectiveness of ultrasound imaging.</p></p>
