<p><p>Adhesives for electronics come in two main types: non-electrically conductive and electrically conductive. Non-electrically conductive adhesives are used to bond electronics components without interfering with electrical signals. They provide excellent insulation and protection against moisture and other environmental factors. On the other hand, electrically conductive adhesives are designed to create electrical connections between components while also providing strong adhesion. They are commonly used in applications where electrical conductivity is required, such as in circuit boards and semiconductor devices.</p></p>
