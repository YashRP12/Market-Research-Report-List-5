<p><p>Blood sugar monitoring equipment market can be divided into two main types: minimally invasive and non-invasive. Minimally invasive devices typically require a small blood sample, usually obtained by pricking the finger. These devices are commonly used by individuals with diabetes to monitor their blood sugar levels at home. Non-invasive devices, on the other hand, do not require any blood sample and instead use alternative methods such as sensors or infrared technology to measure blood sugar levels. Both types of devices play a vital role in managing diabetes and improving healthcare outcomes.</p></p>
