<p><p>Blood apheresis collection systems are used to separate components of blood for therapeutic purposes. There are two main types of systems: centrifugation and membrane filtration. Centrifugation systems use centrifugal force to separate blood components based on their density, while membrane filtration systems use semi-permeable membranes to separate components based on size. Both types of systems are used in hospitals, blood banks, and research facilities for procedures like blood donation, plasma exchange, and cell therapy.</p></p>
